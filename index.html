About Me

I am a senior Aerospace Engineering student at the University of Washington, graduating in June 2026, with a strong passion for structural design, thermal analysis, and space systems. My academic and project experience spans satellite structures, deployable origami-inspired mechanisms, and CubeSat development, with a focus on applying FEA, CAD, and dynamic modeling to solve complex aerospace challenges.

Through internships at Whisper Aero, CEM Corporation, and the Advanced Rocket Research Center, I have gained hands-on experience in mechanical design, propulsion testing, and automation. I am proficient in MATLAB, Python, SolidWorks, Creo, and Ansys, and skilled in practical engineering techniques such as composite layups, hardware integration, and experimental testing. My leadership roles in the Design/Build/Fly team and Boeing-sponsored CubeSat capstone project have also strengthened my teamwork, mentorship, and project management abilities.

My long-term goal is to advance in the fields of spacecraft structures and propulsion, leveraging interdisciplinary knowledge to contribute to satellite systems, space exploration, and sustainable aerospace innovation.
