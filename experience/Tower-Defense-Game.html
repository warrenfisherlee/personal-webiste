2025 ASME SPDC Mechanisms

I collaborated with one teammate to design and build a device for the 2025 ASME SPDC competition that automatically classifies balls of different sizes and materials, delivering them quickly and accurately to six designated packaging stations.

V1 Mechanisms (Sort By Color)

A funnel is placed at the top of the machine to separate large and small balls, guiding them through different channels into the color recognition module. Since the module performs best in darkness, a cover is added above the funnel to minimize light interference. After classification, the balls pass through a three-way conduit to their designated color and size compartments. At the final delivery point, a gate is opened to allow the balls to roll out smoothly.

V2 Mechanisms (Sort By Rebound Height)

A funnel is mounted at the top of the machine to separate large and small balls, which then drop freely onto a metal plate. The rebound height is used to distinguish and classify their material before guiding them into the corresponding channels. Upon reaching the target point, a gate is opened to release the balls smoothly from the machine.
