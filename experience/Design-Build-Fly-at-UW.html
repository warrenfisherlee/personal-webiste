Design Build Fly at Univerity of Washington

As Stability & Tail Configuration Lead for the University of Washington’s Design-Build-Fly team, I completed tail sizing using OpenVSP and hand calculations, optimizing lift–drag performance to improve aerodynamic efficiency. Leveraging XFLR5, I enhanced dynamic stability and aerodynamic performance by 10% for short-distance takeoff. I also supported the design of a wind tunnel model for aerodynamic testing and validation, and designed deployable landing gear components using mill, lathe, 3D printing, and CNC machining, validating structural integrity through analysis and drop testing. Additionally, I gained hands-on experience in carbon fiber composite layup and laser cutting to produce high-quality aircraft parts.
