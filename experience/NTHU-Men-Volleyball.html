Image recognition

The vision system is divided into two sub-projects: one employs OpenCV with a fisheye camera for environment recognition, while the other utilizes YOLO training to develop a dedicated harbor recognition system.

Image-recognition by OpenCV

We developed an OpenCV-based function to recognize both the coordinates and the letters on blocks. The process begins with a color mask to extract the region of interest, followed by corner detection and shape analysis to identify the correct letter and compute the block’s centroid. A coordinate transformation matrix is then applied to convert image coordinates into world coordinates, which are fed back to the robot for control tasks.

Image-recognition by YOLO

A large image dataset was constructed with labeled objects, followed by data augmentation through programmatically varying lighting, color tone, and orientation to enhance recognition confidence. The augmented dataset was then used to train YOLO, resulting in a model whose detection confidence consistently exceeded 60%.
