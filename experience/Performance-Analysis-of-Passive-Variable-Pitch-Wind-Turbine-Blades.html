Performance Analysis of Passive Variable-Pitch Wind Turbine Blades

Conducted experimental research on passive variable-pitch wind turbine blades, designing an aerodynamic mechanism that adjusts blade pitch without servomotors to optimize startup and power output. Demonstrated up to 180% higher efficiency than fixed-pitch blades at low wind speeds, validating the design’s suitability for small- and medium-scale turbines in variable wind environments. This project earned 1st Place in the Taipei Science Fair, 3rd Place in the Taiwan National Science Fair , and Golden Award in the KidWind Challenge.
